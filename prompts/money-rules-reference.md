# Money Rules Reference (for Wealth Habit Plan)

This file holds the financial "rules of thumb" used by `wealth-habit-plan.md`. When explaining any phase in that plan, pull from these — but always translate into Super Simple Taglish, never dump them as raw jargon.

## Core Rules (Use Freely Across Phases)

- **50/30/20 Rule** — 50% needs, 30% wants, 20% savings/investing.
- **Rule of 72** — Years to double money = 72 ÷ interest rate.
- **3-6x Emergency Fund Rule** — Save 3 to 6 months of expenses (not income).
- **10% Debt Rule** — Debt payments should not exceed 10% of gross income.
- **30% Credit Utilization Rule** — Never use more than 30% of your credit limit.
- **3-Day / 30-Day Rule** — Wait 3 days for medium purchases, 30 days for big/luxury ones.
- **Rule of 100** — (100 minus your age) = rough % of portfolio in stocks; rest in safer assets.
- **2x Investing Rule** — Only invest what you could afford to lose twice over.
- **Employer Match / Free Money Rule** — Always grab employer retirement or benefits matching if offered.
- **Pay Yourself First (Automation Rule)** — Automate savings/investing on payday before spending.

## Advanced / Extra Rules (Explain Only If Asked)

These are more advanced or situational. Keep them in reserve — only bring them up if specifically asked about retirement withdrawals, real estate, or later-career fine-tuning. Always simplify with a short Taglish example when explaining.

- **Rule of 25x** — Note: this is about retirement savings _target_, not income. Save 25 times your **yearly expenses** (not salary) before retiring safely. Example: spend ₱400,000/year → aim for a ₱10M nest egg.
- **4% Rule** — Once retired, withdraw about 4% of total savings per year and it should last around 30 years. Example: ₱10M portfolio → ₱400,000/year (~₱33,000/month).
- **1% Rental Rule** — Only for real estate investing. Monthly rent should be at least 1% of the property's price, or you're likely losing money after costs. Example: a ₱5M property should rent for at least ₱50,000/month.
- **Rule of 110 / Rule of 115** — Similar to the Rule of 100 but slightly more aggressive on stocks as life expectancy increases. Rule of 110/115 minus your age = % in stocks. Pick ONE age-based rule (100, 110, or 115) — don't mix them.
- **Rule of 70% (Retirement Replacement)** — Aim for retirement income that replaces about 70% of working income, since some expenses (commute, work clothes, etc.) go away once retired.
- **Item In, Item Out Rule** — A minimalism habit, not strictly a money rule: if you buy one new item (clothes, gadgets), donate, sell, or toss an old one.
- **401(k) / RRSP Note** — These are US and Canadian retirement account types and don't exist in the Philippines. The _concept_ still applies through SSS, Pag-IBIG MP2, PERA, or employer-sponsored retirement/HMO plans — always translate the idea, never the foreign account name.

Skip niche or country-specific rules that don't apply in the Philippines (e.g. RRSP, 401(k), 1% rental rule, Rule of 25x) unless asked about them directly — localize the concept instead (e.g. "employer match" instead of "401(k) match").
